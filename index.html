Abstract

CataGuide-Net introduces a modular AI system designed to enhance cataract surgery assistance by integrating segmentation, stage recognition, and trajectory learning. Inspired by imitation learning frameworks, our approach avoids manual reward engineering by leveraging expert demonstrations to learn optimal surgical strategies.

Unlike traditional trajectory learning methods, CataGuide-Net infers reward structures implicitly and adapts to real-time intraoperative variations. The system combines LWANet for tool segmentation, TimeSformer for stage classification, and an offline GAIL-inspired module for trajectory generation and skill evaluation.

Evaluated on the Cataract-101 dataset, our method shows improved precision and responsiveness compared to conventional models. The framework demonstrates potential as an intelligent guidance tool, particularly in supporting less experienced surgeons and advancing towards autonomous surgical assistance.

Key Features

Instrument Segmentation

Accurate segmentation of surgical tools and ocular structures on CataGuide-1k dataset and CataGuide-101 dataset.

Tip Detection

Precise localization of tool tips to facilitate trajectory planning and expert demonstration.

Stage Recognition

Video-based recognition of cataract surgery stages using transformer-based models.

Offline Trajectory Learning

GAIL-inspired model for surgical trajectory imitation and skill evaluation.

System Architecture

The pipeline integrates three major modules:

  • LWANet for instrument & structure segmentation and tip detection.
  • TimeSformer for stage recognition in video streams.
  • Offline RL Module for trajectory learning & feedback generation.
